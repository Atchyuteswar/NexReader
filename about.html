Our Mission

Why NexReader exists

Empower every reader to engage more deeply with knowledge — no matter the format, no matter the device, no matter where they are in the world.

Reading is one of humanity's oldest and most powerful technologies. Yet in 2024, the software we use to read felt frozen in 2010. PDF viewers that couldn't annotate. E-readers that couldn't open half the formats you owned. Libraries scattered across six different apps. We believed readers deserved better.

We started NexReader with a radical belief: that your entire reading life — every format, every annotation, every insight — should live in one place, backed by intelligence that actually understands what you're reading. Not a search engine over your books. An actual reading companion.

What We Stand For

Our values

📖

Readers First

Every decision starts with one question: does this make reading better? Not engagement metrics, not growth hacks — just the experience of the person holding the tablet.

🔒

Privacy by Default

Your reading is intimate. Your books, your annotations, your questions to the AI — none of it is our data. We build features that work without compromising what you keep private.

♾️

Universal Access

Every format, every language, every ability level. A reader in Hyderabad with dyslexia deserves the same quality experience as a researcher in Oxford using a stylus.

🌱

Long-term Thinking

We're building an app you'll use for decades, not a startup that flips in three years. Sustainable revenue, no VC pressure to sell your data, and a roadmap measured in years.

🤝

Radical Honesty

We'll tell you when a feature isn't ready yet, when we made a mistake, and what we're actually working on. No vague roadmaps, no fake feature announcements.

⚡

Craft Over Speed

We ship slowly and ship well. One delightful, polished feature beats five half-baked ones. The reading experience is too important to rush.

Our Journey

From idea to reality

Jan 2025 · Inception
The idea is born
Arjun and Priya quit their jobs after spending a weekend frustrated with every available reading app. They sketch NexReader on a whiteboard in a Hyderabad café.
Apr 2025 · First Build
Core reader ships internally
First working prototype — EPUB and PDF rendering, basic sync, and the first version of the reading view. The team grows to 5 people.
Aug 2025 · AI Alpha
Ask the Book goes live
The AI engine — including Ask the Book, summarisation, and vocabulary intelligence — is tested internally. 500 books processed in the first week.
Nov 2025 · Beta
Closed beta with 5,000 users
App Store TestFlight and Google Play Internal Testing launch. 50,000 books uploaded in the first month. App Store rating: 4.8 stars.
Q1 2026 · Public Launch
NexReader 1.0 — available now
Free, Pro, Scholar, and Enterprise plans. iOS and Android tablet apps. The app we always wished existed — finally shipped.
