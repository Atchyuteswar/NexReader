Ask the Book
Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind
Yuval Noah Harari · Chapter 7 · p.121
"The Cognitive Revolution kickstarted history about 70,000 years ago. From that point forward, Homo sapiens developed the capacity for fictional thinking…"
Why does Harari say fiction was revolutionary?
Harari argues that the ability to believe in shared fictions — myths, nations, money, religion — is what allowed Homo sapiens to cooperate in massive groups. Animals cooperate based on actual social bonds; only humans can cooperate with strangers through shared narrative. This is the "cognitive revolution" that no other species achieved.

Under the Hood

How the AI works

A layered intelligence that respects your privacy while delivering real depth.

01
Book is indexed on upload
When you add a book, it is parsed and chunked into semantically meaningful segments. An embedding is generated for each chunk — on-device for private books, or cloud-assisted for large volumes.
02
Your question is understood
Your query is embedded and compared against all book chunks using cosine similarity via pgvector. The most relevant passages are retrieved — this is retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).
03
Answer is grounded in the text
The LLM is given only the retrieved passages as context, and is instructed to answer from the text alone. It cannot hallucinate because it's constrained to what's on the page.
04
Citations are always attached
Every response includes the page numbers and chapter of the passages used. You can tap any citation to jump directly to that location in the book.
SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE
📖 Your Book
↓ parsed & chunked
🔢 Embeddings (pgvector)
↓ cosine similarity search
🎯 Relevant Passages Retrieved
↓ grounded prompt
🧠 LLM (Claude / GPT-4o)
↓ streamed response
✦ Answer + Page Citations
