AI · Ask the Book
Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind
Yuval Noah Harari · Chapter 7 · p.121
"The Cognitive Revolution kickstarted history about 70,000 years ago. From that point forward, there are no more significant revolutions in the organisation of our world…"
Why does Harari call it a "revolution"?
Harari uses "revolution" because the Cognitive Revolution marks a sudden, unprecedented leap in Homo sapiens' ability to think in abstract, fictional terms — enabling language, cooperation at scale, and culture. This wasn't gradual evolution but a threshold crossing that changed everything about how humans organised society.
↗ Sources: p.117, p.121, p.128
